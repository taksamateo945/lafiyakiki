Barbara Alyn Woods is an American actress. Barbara Alyn Woods is well known for One Tree Hill, Striptease, and Star Trek: The Next Generation.

Early Life and Childhood

Barbara was born on March 11, 1962, in Chicago, Illinois, U.S. She is 61 years old. The actress is usually called by her nickname Babs. Her father is Allen Woods but there is no detail about her mother.

Soon after she was born, the family migrated to Darien. There, she spent the rest of her growing-up years and attended Hinsdale South High School.

Furthermore, the actress received a theatre scholarship, on which she joined Northern Illinois University. At this university, she further honed her skills as an actress.

Following her college graduation, she kept doing theatres and at some point, she also started auditioning for roles in films and TV. Eventually, she made her television debut in her mid-20s.

Career

Barbara has acted professionally from the year 1988. She is well recognized for portraying Deborah “Deb” Scott on the WB/CW teen drama series One Tree Hill. Moreover, she starred in the show for six years from 2003 and appeared in the final season in 2012. Due to the format change of the show, she only appeared in four episodes of season 5 but in season 6, she returned as a series regular.

This beautiful actress played the lead role in the 1993 USA Network prime-time soap opera Eden, which was later cancelled after 26 episodes. Furthermore, she posed nude for the June 1993 issue of Playboy as part of a promotion for the television series called Eden.

Moreover, she is also known for portraying the role of Diane Szalinski on Honey, I Shrunk the Kids: The TV Show which ran from the year 1997 to 2000. In the early 1990s, she starred in various films such as The Terror Within II (1991), Circuitry Man (1990), Delusion (1991), Flesh and Bone (1993), The Waterdance (1992),  Ghoulies IV (1994) and Frankie Starlight (1995). Also, in 1996, she appeared in Demi Moore’s film Striptease.

In addition, the actress also has appeared in guest roles on numerous TV series which include Star Trek: The Next Generation, Picket Fences, Married… with Children, Desperate Housewives, The Golden Girls, Seinfeld, Murder, She Wrote, Touched by an Angel, Dream On, and was recurring on ABC’s The Gates in 2010.
